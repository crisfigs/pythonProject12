Professor, would you be interested in the following one time mentorship opportunity?

‘‘A-Drink-with-a-Prof’’

Hi,


We are a group of faculty and students at the UVA and we would like to propose the UVA to set up a simple and easy mentorship program to bring together faculty and students. We explain the program below. Before setting it up, we would like to evaluate how many professors would be interested in participating.

What?


A one time mentorship opportunity at a pub nearby campus. Students and professors are paired and awarded a voucher for a (non-alcoholic) drink.


When?



The date is agreed by the student and the professor. Vouchers are awarded for an afternoon meeting (when both students and professors are less busy).


Who?



The first edition of the program targets male undergraduate students studying at the UVA. Professor and students are matched within faculty.


Please let us know if you would like to participate using the blue buttons below. If you sign up, we will include you in the pool of professors to be matched with students in the first edition of our program.

This program will allow you to (for professors):

  • Get to know your students better.

  • Provide valuable career guidance like discuss career options or share strategies for a successful career.

  • Showcase your own research projects and gain fresh perspectives.

  • Help you and your student feel more connected to the UVA.

  • Help you feel more enthusiastic about your role.



Tips for conversation during the “drink” (for students)


  • Share information about yourself that you are comfortable talking about. This helps professors to get to know you. Where are you from? What brought you to the UVA? Why did you select your bachelor program?

  • Share about the subjects you have taken: what you enjoyed, what may have been challenging for you, or what surprised you.

  • Ask what courses are related to your interests and which they would recommend.

  • Ask about research opportunities, advice on your career direction, or recommended graduate programs.

  • Be certain to be respectful of their time and to thank them for making time to meet with you.
